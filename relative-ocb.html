Exploring Relative OCB

A new perspective on organizational citizenship behavior

Key Findings

Results from the study reveal that employees who engage in relatively higher OCB enjoy more positive evaluations from supervisors. These employees are often perceived as more committed and loyal, which can translate into career advantages. However, the relational nature of this concept means that some employees may look less supportive simply because their peers are more active in helping behaviors.

Conclusion

By shifting the focus from absolute to relative measures of citizenship behaviors, the concept of Relative OCB provides a richer lens for understanding how employees are evaluated in organizational settings. It highlights both the promise and potential pitfalls of recognizing contributions in a comparative framework.
